2. SECTION 7520 RATE - ASSUMPTIONS & TREATMENT
================================================================================

DEFAULT RATE: 4.2% (0.042)
  - IRS Section 7520 hurdle rate used for valuation
  - Determined by month and year from IRS tables
  - Default 4.2% is mid-range historical rate (2015-2026 average)
  - Source: pipeline_artifacts/retrieval/parsed/S007.txt (IRC §7520)

RATE VALIDATION:
  - Must be > 0 and <= 20% (0.20)
  - Configurable when calling run_deterministic_model(section_7520_rate=X)
  - Used to calculate:
    * GRAT annuity payment (present value of annuity formula)
    * CRAT charitable deduction (present value discount factor)

RATE LOCKDOWN:
  - Once model runs, the rates used are embedded in output JSON
  - Rates are NOT extracted from S007 chunks on each run
  - User must explicitly specify rate or use default
  - This ensures determinism and traceability

3. GRAT - GRANTOR RETAINED ANNUITY TRUST ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

INITIAL CONTRIBUTION:
  Amount: $16,000,000 (from liquidity event)
  Tax treatment: No gift tax (zeroed-out GRAT model)
  Deposited into: Irrevocable trust with qualified annuity interest

TERM LENGTH:
  Assumption: 10 years (fixed)
  Rationale: Standard GRAT term; minimizes mortality risk while enabling growth
  IRC reference: §2702 (qualified interest)

ANNUITY CALCULATION:
  Formula: PV of Annuity = Contribution / [(1 - (1+r)^-n) / r]
    Where:
      r = section_7520_rate (4.2%)
      n = term_years (10)
      Contribution = $16,000,000
  
  Computed annuity: $1,992,344.36 per year
  Total paid to grantor over 10 years: $19,923,443.60
  
  Assumption: Grantor survives the full 10-year term
    - If grantor dies during term, full trust corpus is included in estate
    - This is a standard GRAT mortality risk (not hedged in model)
  
  Assumption: Annuity payments are made annually on a fixed date
    - Not semi-annual or other intervals
    - Grantor must survive each payment date to receive
  
  Assumption: Annuity is NOT adjusted for inflation
    - Fixed payment amount for entire term
    - This differs from some GRAT structures that adjust for CPI

TRUST GROWTH ASSUMPTION:
  Expected annual growth rate: 5% (0.05, fixed)
  Rationale: Moderate mid-range assumption for diversified portfolio
  Treatment: Applied year-over-year to trust value
  
  Alternative scenarios NOT modeled:
    - Negative/recession growth
    - Market-conditional growth
    - Asset-specific growth rates
  
  Simulation algorithm:
    Value_start_of_year = previous_value × (1.05)
    Value_end_of_year = (value_start_of_year - $1,992,344.36) × max to $0
    
    This order matters: growth is applied BEFORE annuity payment

REMAINDER AT TERM:
  Projected value: $1,002,820.77 (at 5% growth)
  Passes to: Children named as remainder beneficiaries
  Treatment: Removed from grantor's taxable estate (no §2033 inclusion)
  
  Assumptions embedded in remainder:
    - Children survive the grantor (required for bequest)
    - Trust assets appreciate as projected
    - No extraordinary trust expenses deplete corpus
    - Trustee makes standard investment decisions

GIFT TAX TREATMENT:
  Taxable gift: $0.00 (zeroed-out GRAT)
  Assumption: Annuity PV ≈ Remainder PV at inception
  IRC basis: §2702(a)(2)(A) - qualified annuity interest exemption
  
  Why zero gift tax?
    - The grantor's retained annuity interest is excluded from gift value
    - Using §7520 rate ensures the retained interest value = initial corpus
    - Therefore, taxable gift = corpus - retained interest value = $0

ESTATE TAX SAVINGS:
  Amount removed from estate: $1,002,820.77 (remainder only)
  Estate tax rate: 40% (from 2015 context)
  Estate tax saved: $401,128.31
  
  This assumes:
    - Grantor is subject to federal estate tax (estate > exemptions)
    - 40% marginal estate tax rate applies (2015 law)
    - No state-level estate taxes considered
    - Remainder is not re-gifted by children

4. CRAT - CHARITABLE REMAINDER ANNUITY TRUST ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

INITIAL CONTRIBUTION:
  Amount: $16,000,000 (from liquidity event)
  Tax treatment: Assets removed from grantor's ownership
  Deposited into: Irrevocable trust with charitable remainder interest

TERM LENGTH:
  Assumption: 20 years (fixed)
  Rationale: Longer than GRAT to accrue substantial remainder for charity
  IRC reference: §664(a) - minimum 20% charitable remainder at end of term

ANNUITY CALCULATION:
  Payout rate: 5% of initial corpus (fixed)
  Annual payment: $16,000,000 × 0.05 = $800,000
  Total paid to grantor over 20 years: $16,000,000
  
  Assumptions:
    - Payout is fixed dollar amount (not percentage of current value)
    - Grantor (or named income beneficiary) receives payment each year
    - Payout must be at least 5% (IRS minimum for CRT status) ✓
    - Payout must be no more than 50% (IRS maximum for CRT)
    - Grantor survives to receive payments (no death assumption)

INCOME TAX TREATMENT (DONOR):
  Upfront charitable deduction: $4,934,444.99 (estimated)
  
  Calculation method:
    1. Project remainder at 4% growth (conservative for deduction)
    2. Discount back using §7520 rate (4.2%) for 20 years
    3. Result represents PV of charitable interest
  
  Assumptions:
    - Donor is subject to federal income tax
    - Deduction is limited to 50% of AGI (cash contributions)
    - Donor can carry forward excess deduction for 5 years
    - This model does NOT account for income tax complexity
    - This is an ESTIMATE; actual deduction requires IRS valuation tables

TRUST GROWTH ASSUMPTION:
  Expected annual growth rate: 5% (0.05, fixed)
  Rationale: Same as GRAT for comparison fairness
  Treatment: Applied year-over-year to trust value
  
  Simulation algorithm (same as GRAT):
    Value_start_of_year = previous_value × (1.05)
    Value_end_of_year = (value_start_of_year - $800,000) × max to $0
    
    At 5% growth = annual growth amount, and 5% payout = fixed amount:
    Growth and payout nearly offset each other.

REMAINDER AT TERM:
  Projected value: $16,000,000.00 (essentially unchanged)
  Passes to: Designated charitable beneficiary
  Treatment: Removed from grantor's taxable estate
  
  Assumptions:
    - Charity designated upfront and cannot be changed
    - Charity must be qualified (IRC §501(c)(3) or equivalent)
    - Trustee allocates all remaining assets to charity
    - No portion reverts to grantor or family

ESTATE TAX TREATMENT - CRITICAL CHANGE (FIXED MARCH 5, 2026):
  
  PREVIOUS INCORRECT ASSUMPTION:
    - Estate reduction: $16,000,000 (incorrect)
    - Estate tax saved: $6,400,000 (incorrect)
    - Rationale: "Full corpus removed from estate"
  
  CORRECTED ASSUMPTION (IRC §2036):
    - Estate reduction: $4,934,444.99 (PV of charitable deduction)
    - Estate tax saved: $1,973,777.97 (deduction × 40%)
    - Rationale: Under IRC §2036, grantor's retained income interest
                 means FULL CORPUS is INCLUDED in estate at death.
                 Only the present value of the charitable remainder
                 (= charitable deduction) reduces the taxable estate.
  
  IRC §2036 Logic:
    - If grantor retains any income interest → assets included in estate
    - CRAT donor retains annuity payments for 20 years → included in estate
    - BUT: Charitable deduction offsets the included amount
    - Net estate reduction = only the deduction amount
    - This is why CRAT saves LESS estate tax than GRAT (which has no inclusion)

ASSUMPTION CRITICAL TO ALL COMPARISONS:
  - GRAT removes remainder from estate (IRC §2036 does not apply - qualified interest)
  - CRAT includes full corpus but deducts charitable portion
  - This difference drives all downstream comparison results

8. WHAT IS NOT MODELED (OUT OF SCOPE)
================================================================================

MODEL LIMITATIONS:

  Tax Complexity:
    - Grantor trust income tax treatment (IRC §671-683) - not modeled
    - Ordinary income, capital gains, and AMT computations - not modeled
    - State income or estate taxes - not modeled
    - Generation-skipping transfer tax (GST) - not modeled
  
  Trust Administration:
    - Annual trustee fees and expenses - not modeled
    - Trust distribution requirements - not modeled
    - Trustee discretion in distributions - not modeled
    - Trust protector provisions - not modeled
  
  Market Scenarios:
    - Volatility or downside risk - not modeled
    - Recession/negative growth scenarios - not modeled
    - Inflation impact on fixed annuities - not modeled
    - Asset-class-specific returns - not modeled
  
  Mortality & Life Events:
    - Grantor mortality during term (critical for GRATs) - not modeled
    - Beneficiary mortality - not modeled
    - Divorce or family changes - not modeled
    - Incapacity/guardianship - not modeled
  
  Legal & Regulatory:
    - Changes to tax law post-2015 - not modeled
    - State law variations - not modeled
    - Non-charitable trust restriction rules - not modeled
    - IRS audit outcomes or adjustments - not modeled
  
  Integration:
    - Interaction with other estate planning (powers of appointment, etc.) - not modeled
    - Coordination with life insurance - not modeled
    - Qualified personal residence trust (QPRT) - not modeled
    - Charitable lead trusts (CLT) - not modeled

    Why this matters:
      GRAT removes remainder from taxable estate (qualified interest under §2702)
      CRAT includes full corpus but deducts charitable portion under §2036
