## Bottom Line

- **GRAT wins for family wealth transfer.** It passes ~$3.7M to the client's children with zero gift tax (the zeroed-out GRAT technique exploits the low 7520 rate of 1.96%).
- **CRAT wins for estate tax reduction and charitable giving.** It cuts the taxable estate by $7.6M, saves an additional ~$1.57M in estate tax vs. the GRAT, and donates the full $16M corpus to charity.
- **Neither is obviously superior** — the right choice depends on the client's priority:
  - Prioritise **children → GRAT**
  - Prioritise **charity + estate tax savings → CRAT**
